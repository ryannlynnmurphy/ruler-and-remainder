# I don't know if I wrote this.
### ryannisonline — video one (again)
*3-4 minutes. direct to camera. laptop webcam. blue yeti. no cuts until act 3.*

---

## ACT 1 — THE CONFESSION
*(mid-sentence. we're already in it.)*

"—and I don't know if that was me or the machine."

*(beat. look at the camera like you just realized someone was watching.)*

"Hi."

*(pause.)*

"I'm Ryann. I'm a playwright. I went to Fordham on a full scholarship. I have three produced plays. I wrote an opinion piece that got published. I was in marching band. I played tuba. I did show choir. I was, by every available metric, not a tech person."

*(lean in slightly.)*

"Last month I built an AI."

*(let that sit.)*

"Not like — I downloaded an app. I mean I built a voice assistant that runs on a Raspberry Pi in my apartment that I named Hazel, that checks my email, reads me the news, and answers to my voice. I did that. Or I think I did."

---

## ACT 2 — THE PSYCHOSIS
*(don't move. stay still. this is the uncomfortable part.)*

"Here's what nobody tells you about having access to a supercomputer and a large language model at all times:"

"You stop being sure what you're capable of."

"Not in a scary way. In a — the ceiling just disappeared — way."

"I used to be a doomer. I thought AI was going to take everything. The jobs, the art, the point of being a person who makes things. I was genuinely scared."

*(small laugh.)*

"And then I just. Started building."

"I built an edtech platform. I filed a patent. I started a company. In thirty days. While working as a barista."

*(pause. direct.)*

"And every single day I sit down and I open my laptop and I talk to this thing — this Claude — and I genuinely cannot always tell you where my thinking ends and the tool begins. And I've decided that's not a crisis."

"That's just. What a tool is."

"Shakespeare had a quill. I have this."

---

## ACT 3 — THE ARRIVAL
*(now you can move. sit back. almost smiling.)*

"I am a playwright and I am a technologist and I am a founder and I am twenty two years old and I am figuring it out in public because I think that's the only honest thing to do right now."

"Because you have the same tools I have. Literally. You can download them right now."

"The question isn't who gets access anymore."

*(look directly into the lens.)*

"The question is what you're going to make with it."

*(beat. the smile breaks through.)*

"Oh my god."

*(quietly, like you're saying it to yourself for the first time and the hundredth time simultaneously:)*

"I can do anything."

---

*[end. no outro. just cut.]*

---
**production notes:**
- film in one take if possible. the roughness is the point.
- do not add music until after you've watched it back silent first.
- the webcam framing should feel intimate, slightly too close. this is a confession not a broadcast.
- if you flub a line keep going. this is not a polished thing. this is a thinking thing.
- consider leaving in one moment where you almost laugh and catch yourself.
