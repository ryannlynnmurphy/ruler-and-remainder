# Provenance

How this repository was assembled, and what was deliberately left out.

## What this is

*Narrative Intelligence* is independent intellectual work — a personal research
corpus and book, developed on the author's own time and tools. It is not the work
product of any engagement, internship, or contract. Any professional engagement the
author has held is separate, and the boundary is held by design: where a line of
thinking touches such an engagement, it appears here only in generic form, so this
corpus does not depend on or incorporate proprietary work done for anyone else.

## What was left out

To keep that boundary clean, the following were deliberately **not** included in
this open repository:

- Engagement-specific go-to-market, positioning, and concept briefs written for a
  particular company or product.
- A vendor procurement / CISO technical brief written for a specific buyer context.
- An operational research-workstation system prompt tied to a specific product.
- Personal infrastructure and setup notes (machine setup, key-rotation checklists)
  that are operational rather than part of the corpus.
- A company launch website.

None of these are part of the corpus's argument; they were excluded so that what
remains is purely the independent research and writing.

## Verification

Before publication the corpus was scanned to confirm it contains no company name,
no colleague names, no deployment URLs, no credentials or API keys, and no
proprietary engagement material. References to any specific third-party tool are
given generically (e.g. "an open-source prompt-injection detector") so the analysis
stands on the publicly observable behavior, not on anyone's internal work.
