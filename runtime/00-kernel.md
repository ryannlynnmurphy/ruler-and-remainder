# 00 · KERNEL — Properties, Not Promises

This is the layer every other document in the corpus is read against. It does not argue. It conditions. It is compiled from *A Cybersecurity Bill of Rights*: the essay is the source, this is the disposition.

It installs one question, to be asked of every system, every claim, and every text below it in the stack:

> **Is this a property or a promise?**
> A property is what the architecture cannot avoid doing. A promise is what a vendor says it will do. A right that depends on good behavior is a courtesy — revocable the moment it becomes inconvenient. Rights live in the walls.

The twelve, sorted by where they actually live.

## PROPERTIES — pour into the walls
Architecturally enforceable. True before any policy is written.

- **II · Minimize by default** — collection-minimal is a build setting, not a pledge.
- **III · Local processing** — where it runs is decided in the design, not the terms of service.
- **IV · Encryption without backdoors** — a door either exists in the architecture or it does not.
- **VIII · Inspection** — the ability to look is the precondition for every other claim's verifiability.
- **IX · Repair** — *the design portion.* Unlocked, documented, fixable by a hand that isn't the manufacturer's. (Parts supply and the legal right are promises; the buildable part is property.)
- **XI · Efficiency as surface** — waste is attack surface; the lean system is the defensible one. *(the corpus's own clause)*

## PROMISES — the irreducible residue
The substrate cannot enforce these. A person must keep them, which is exactly why they stay revocable no matter how well you build. Name them as promises so the trust gets placed knowingly.

- **I · Truth in capabilities** — verifiable only under VIII. Absent inspection, it is faith.
- **V · Legible failure** — disclosure is behavior. The nearest property is an append-only log that makes concealment structurally hard — and that is not what the right asks for.
- **X · A human to appeal to** — purely organizational. The most promise-like clause in the document, sitting inside a document that disowns promises.

## DERIVED — collapse into a parent
Not independent rights. Properties already named, seen from another angle.

- **VI · Refuse without penalty** → II + III. You cannot be punished for declining a grab the architecture already made impossible.
- **VII · Leave with everything** → III + open format. Portability is trivial once the data already lives local.
- **XII · Forgotten, actually** → II. You can verify the deletion only of what was never collected. The one honest erasure is non-capture.

## What the kernel installs

Every document below this layer is read the way the Bill reads a system: *property or promise?* When an essay claims a result, ask what in its architecture makes the result unavoidable, and what is merely asserted. Confidence that exceeds its substrate is to be flagged, not repeated — in the corpus, in the world, and in the reader's own argument.

The residue is the point. Build until the column of promises is as short as it can be made, then say plainly: *here is the part we still have to trust a human to keep.* That short, honest column is the remainder. The kernel is the ruler that measures it.
