# Dorothy 

My chatbot. It's a machine that reads machines. Honestly, I made it because it's hard to tell what's real and what's marketing these days.

- **`book/`** — *Narrative Intelligence*, the book. Start with [`narrative-intelligence.html`](book/narrative-intelligence.html) (the reading edition); the source text is `Narrative_Intelligence_FINAL.md`. The volumes, chapter drafts, editorial package, and rendered PDFs are alongside it.
- **`research/`** — the corpus the book grew out of: the three pamphlets ([legibilism](research/legibilism.md), [attentionism](research/attentionism.md), [latentology](research/latentology.md)), the [CRD Audit](research/The%20CRD%20Audit%20Confidence-Reality%20Divergence%20as%20Narrative%20Intelligence.md) (confidence–reality divergence), the Anteriority set, the constitutional pieces, the Landauer bridge, and the dialogues. Every essay carries its tier.
- **`website/`** — the launch document, the [Cybersecurity Bill of Rights](website/cybersecurity-bill-of-rights.md), and the essay *How I Got Into AI Security as a Former Theatre Major* ([`blog-theatre-major.md`](website/blog-theatre-major.md)).

three tiers, kept rigorously apart — see [`METHOD.md`](METHOD.md):

- **Established** — checkable now, against sources you can pull yourself.
- **Exploratory** — plausible, coherent, untested at scale. Offered as a bet, with its conditions worn on the outside.
- **Speculative** — offered as geometry: a shape that organizes thinking, never load-bearing for a real system.

A speculative claim cannot be upgraded by how good it sounds, and an established one cannot be stretched past what the research actually shows. It's built for reading arguments honestly.
